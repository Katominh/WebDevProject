Saturn

Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system, famous for its stunning ring system. It is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: ~120,500 km (9.5 times Earth's diameter)
  • Gravity: 10.4 m/s2 (about 91% of Earth's gravity)
  • Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and othergases
  • Moons: 146 confirmed moons, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solarsystem
  • Temperature: -178℃ (varies depending on depth in the atmosphere)

Fun Facts

  • Saturn could float on water
    • It has an average density lower than water, meaning if you could find a bathtub big enough, Saturn would float!
  • The rings are super thin
    • Despite stretching 282,000 km wide, they're only 10 to 30 meters thick (thinner than a football field)
  • It has a weird hexagon at its North Pole
    • Saturn's North Pole has a gigantic hexagonal storm, a mystery scientists are still studying!
  • A year on Saturn is nearly 30 Earth years!
    • While Saturn spins fast (one day is equal to 10.7 Earth hours), it takes 29.5 Earth Years to orbit the Sun

Saturn's North Pole Hexagon

  • A massive six-sided jet stream at Saturn' North Pole
  • About 30,000km (20,000 miles) across. It is big enough to fit four Earths inside
  • Winds inside hexagon move at speed of 322 km/h (200mph)
  • The hexagon appears blue in winter and golden in summer, possibly due to chemical reactions in Saturn's atmosphere driven by sunlight
