Saturn

Overview

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. It's also the closest planet to the Sun, orbiting at an average distance of about 57.9 million km (36 million miles)

Key Facts

  • Diameter: ~4,880 km (~3,032 miles)
  • Gravity: ~38% of Earth's gravity
  • Atmosphere: barely has an atmosphere, just a thin exosphere made of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium(This means it can't retain heat)
  • Moons: Phobos & Deimos
  • Temperature: Extreme!, Ranges from -173℃

Fun Facts

  • Mars has the Tallest Volcano in the solar System
    • Olympus Mons is a massive volcano, about three times the height of Mount Everest! It is around 22 km (13.6 miles) tall and 600 km (373 miles) wide—so big it could cover the entire state of Arizona.
  • Mars has the Biggest Canyon in the Solar System
    • Valles Marineris is a giant canyon system that stretches over 4,000 km (2,500 miles)—that's about the width of the United States! It's 10 times longer and 4 times deeper than the Grand Canyon.
  • Mars has "Blue Sunsets"
    • Unlike Earth's red and orange sunsets, Mars has blue sunsets due to the way its thin atmosphere scatters sunlight. The dusty air absorbs red light and lets blue light pass through, creating an eerie cool-toned glow at dusk.
  • Your weight on Mars would be much less
    • Mars' gravity is only 38% of Earth's, so if you weigh 100 lbs (45 kg) on Earth, you'd weigh just 38 lbs (17 kg) on Mars! Time to hit those high jumps!
