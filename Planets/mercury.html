Mercury

Overview

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. It's also the closest planet to the Sun, orbiting at an average distance of about 57.9 million km (36 million miles).

Key Facts

  • Diameter: ~4,880 km (~3,032 miles)
  • Gravity: ~38% of Earth's gravity
  • Atmosphere: barely has an atmosphere, just a thin exosphere made of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium (This means it can't retain heat)
  • Moons: Phobos & Deimos
  • Temperature: Extreme!, Ranges from -173℃

Fun Facts

  • A day on Mercury is Longer than its Year
    • Mercury takes 88 Earth Days to orbit the Sun but 176 Earth days to complete one full day-night cycle. If you lived there, you'd celebrate two birthdays before seeing one full sunrise-to-sunset day!
  • Mercury is shrinking
    • The planet's core is cooling, causing Mercury to shrink over time. This has created massive cliffs (called lobate scarps) that are hundreds of kilometers long.
  • It has ice despite being the closest planet to the sun
    • Mercury's polar craters, which never receive sunlight, have frozen water trapped inside. NASA's MESSENGER spacecraft found this surprising discovery in 2012.
  • Mercury has a tail like a comet
    • Due to solar radiation, Mercury sheds atoms from its surface, creating a thin, glowing tail of sodium gas that extends millions of kilometers into space. It's invisible to the naked eye but can be detected with special instruments!

Mercury's Day vs Year

  • Mercury's Rotation is Extremely slow
    • It takes 59 Earth days for Mercury to complete one full spin on its axis.
  • Mercury's Orbit is SUPER FAST
    • It zips around the sun in just 88 Earth days, the shortest year of any planet in the solar system.
  • 3:2 Spin-Orbit Resonance
    • Unlike most planets, every 3 spins Mercury does on its axis it completes 2 orbits around the sun.
