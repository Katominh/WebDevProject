Mars

Overview

Mars is the 4th planet from the Sun and the 2nd smallest planet in the Solar System after Mercury. Often called the ‚ÄúRed Planet‚Äù, its reddish appearance comes from iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: ~6,779 km (about half the size of earth)
  • Gravity: ~38% of Earth's gravity
  • Atmosphere: Thin and mostly carbon dioxide (CO2)
  • Moons: Phobos & Deimos
  • Temperature: Extremely Cold, Ranging from -140℃ to 30℃ (-220℉ to 86℉)

Fun Facts

  • Mars has the Tallest Volcano in the Solar System
    • Olympus Mons is a massive volcano, about three times the height of Mount Everest! It is around 22 km (13.6 miles) tall and 600 km (373 miles) wide ‚Äî so big it could cover the state of Arizona.
  • Mars has the Biggest Canyon in the Solar System
    • Valles Marineris is a giant canyon that stretches over 4,000 km (2,500 miles) ‚Äî it's about the width of the United States! It's 10 times longer and 4 times deeper than the Grand Canyon.
  • Mars has "Blue Sunsets"
    • Unlike Earth's red and orange sunsets, Mars has blue sunsets due to the way its thin atmosphere scatters sunlight. The dusty air absorbs red light and lets blue light pass through, creating an eerie cool-toned glow at dusk.
  • Your weight on Mars would be much less
    • Mars' gravity is only 38% of Earth's, so if you are 100 lbs (45 kg) on Earth, you'd weigh just 38 lbs (17 kg) on Mars! Time to hit those high jumps!
