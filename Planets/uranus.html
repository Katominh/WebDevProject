Uranus

Overview

Uranus is the 7th planet from the Sun and is known for its pale blue color and unique sideways rotation. It is classified as an ice giant and has a faint ring system.

Key Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km
  • Orbital Period: 84 years
  • Rotation Period: 17 hours
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Moons: 27 (Largest: Titania)

Fun Facts

  • Uranus rotates on its side, possibly due to a massive collision in the past.
  • It has the coldest atmosphere of any planet, with temperatures dropping to -224Â°C.
  • Uranus was the first planet discovered using a telescope, by William Herschel in 1781.
  • It has faint rings that were discovered in 1977.

Uranus's Rings

Uranus has a system of faint rings, which are composed mostly of dark particles, possibly ice and rock.
