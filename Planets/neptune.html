Neptune

Overview

Neptune is the 8th and farthest planet from the Sun in our Solar System. It is a gas giant, known for its deep blue color and extreme winds, the fastest recorded in the Solar System.

Key Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km
  • Orbital Period: 164.8 years
  • Rotation Period: 16 hours
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Moons: 14 (Largest: Triton)

Fun Facts

  • Neptune was the first planet discovered using mathematical predictions.
  • It has the strongest winds in the Solar System, reaching speeds of 2,100 km/h.
  • Neptune's largest moon, Triton, orbits the planet in the opposite direction of its rotation.
  • It appears blue due to methane in its atmosphere, which absorbs red light.

Neptune's Storms

Neptune is home to massive storms, including the famous Great Dark Spot, which was observed by Voyager 2.

This planet's atmosphere is in constant motion, creating turbulent weather patterns.
