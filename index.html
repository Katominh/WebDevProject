Introduction

Welcome to our website! Here, you will learn about many fascinating and exciting facts about our Solar System! We encourage everyone to think like a scientist, explore your curiosity, and discover new things for yourself - beyond the realm of our home planet!

Let's get started with this question: Do you know which planet is closest to Earth? Check this videobelow!

© Video by CGP Grey 2019

Hey look! It's our Sun spinning around! The Sun is the center of our Solar System, and all the planets orbit around it. If somebody asks you, "What is the closest star to Earth?" You can proudly say, "The Sun!" 🌞

Also, did you know that even with all the planets' masses combined, the Sun still takes up 99.86% of the Solar System's total mass?

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest rocky planet in the Solar System.

Venus

Venus is the 2nd planet from the Sun and has a thick, toxic atmosphere.

Earth

Earth is the 3rd planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life.

Mars

Mars is the 4th planet from the Sun and is known as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance.

Ooooh! ~ It's the Main Asteroid Belt! It is located between Mars and Jupiter and is made up of millions of asteroids in various size and shapes.

Although in fictional movies and writings, the Asteroid Belt is often portrayed as a dangerous place to travel through, in reality, the Asteroid Belt is mostly empty space. The average distance between each asteroid is about 600,000 miles apart 🤯 Crazy right?

Have you wondered at one point: "Why are the Giant Gas Planets called 'Giant Gas Planets' in the first place? Why are they considered planets at all?"

They are made of different compositions than the previous 4 planets and are much, MUCH, larger in size - even hundreds of times more massive than Earth 🤔

As you've probably guessed ~

They are called giant gas planets because of their immense size and their gassy composition, mainly hydrogen and helium.

And they are still planets because they orbit around the Sun, just like others!

If the Sun is the size of a soccer ball, then how far would Earth be? 1 meter? 5 meters? 100 meters?

Turns out! The Earth would be the size of a pin and located about ~24 meters away from the Sun!

Jupiter

Jupiter is the 5th planet from the Sun and is the largest planet in the Solar System.

Saturn

Saturn is the 6th planet from the Sun and is known for its prominent ring system.

Uranus

Uranus is the 7th planet from the Sun and is known for its unique sideways rotation.

Neptune

Neptune is the 8th planet from the Sun and is known for its deep blue color.
